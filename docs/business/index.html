Why · The pain

Shipping AI today
takes months, not days.

Most teams stitch together 5+ tools just to launch their first agent. The result: long engineering cycles, vendor lock-in risk, and unclear answers about where your data goes.

⏱️
Engineering bottleneck
Building, hosting, and operating an AI agent takes a senior dev 6–12 weeks before the first customer touches it.
🧩
Vendor sprawl
Builder, runtime, vector DB, observability, billing — each from a different vendor. Five invoices, no integration.
🔒
Compliance unknowns
Where does customer data go? Which provider sees what? Hard to answer when 5 tools sit in the data path.
⚓
Lock-in risk
Built on one vendor's cloud and they pivot, raise prices, or go down — your AI product goes with them.

How · Security & data

Built for trust.

Every layer is designed so you know where your data lives, who can access it, and how it's protected.

🔐
Auth & access
JWT in httpOnly cookies · MFA · SSO (SAML / OIDC) · SCIM provisioning · role-based access control per workspace.
📜
Audit & traces
Every action logged · every LLM call traced · cost dashboard per workspace · export to your SIEM.
🧪
Sandboxed execution
User-provided code runs in isolated VMs · SSRF protection on tool requests · virus scan on uploads.
🌍
Data residency
Pick US / EU / SG regions on cloud · run fully on-prem with self-host · zero outbound calls when air-gapped.
SOC 2 (in progress) GDPR-ready HIPAA-compatible (self-host + BAA) ISO 27001 (roadmap)
